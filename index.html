Brief History

No one has been able to inspire many minds about him, no one has given a most admirable contribution to the development of the techniques of the arts and humanities.

Born in 1452 in Anchiano, a small town near Florence, Leonardo began to approach the artist's career as an apprentice in the workshop of the great painter, Verrocchio. Admired and respected by princes and powerful, has been in the service of Ludovico il Moro in Milan and Giuliano de Medici to the Vatican and was host of the king of France, Francis I. The extraordinary collection of works he has left in the world is surpassed only by projects that did not have time to complete and for which there is no trace in his personal manuscripts.

Interesting Facts
  1. Leonardo loved water: He developed plans for floating snowshoes, a breathing device for underwater exploration, a life preserver, and a diving bell that could attack ships from below. In case one had to.
  2. Leonardo was the first to explain why the sky is blue. (It's because of the way air scatters light.)
  3. And he figured out why the entire moon is dimly visible when it is a thin crescent. Its nightside is lit by light reflected from Earth, which appears 50 times brighter from the moon than the full moon appears here.
  4. An ambidextrous, paranoid dyslexic, Leonardo could draw forward with one hand while writing backward with the other, producing a mirror-image script that others found difficult to read—which was exactly the point.
  5. He crushed intelligent design before anyone even thought of it: His studies of river erosion convinced him that the Earth is much older than the Bible implies, and he argued that falling sea levels—not Noah's Flood—left marine fossils on mountains.
